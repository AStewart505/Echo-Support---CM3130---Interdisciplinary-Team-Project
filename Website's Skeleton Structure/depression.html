    Depression

    Statistics show that 1-4 adults experience mental health problems with the most common issues reported being depression and anxiety. A person may only experience depression once or they may have lapses several times throughout their life.

    Depression is more than feeling sad or unhappy for a few days, the feelings will persist much longer and severity of the symptoms may fluctuate.

    There is no particular cause of depression but varying psychological, physical and social factors will be involved.

    Some symptoms of depression:

    • Feeling overwhelming sadness.
    • Lack of energy / fatigue.
    • Disinterested in activities that were once enjoyable.
    • Suicidal thoughts.
    • Disturbed sleep pattern.
    • Poor or increased appetite.
    • Slow speech.
    • Slow movement.
    • Can affect confidence and self-esteem.

    Some people will talk openly and frankly about their mental illness but for others it is more difficult as many will have come up against discrimination and stigma.

    Look after yourself:

    • You may be feeling lethargic but simple exercise will help lift your mood.
    • Stay in touch with family and friends. Staying connected with others is important.
    • Try and eat healthy foods.
    • Alcohol is a depressant so best avoid or reduce it.
