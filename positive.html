    Think Positive

    “You are what you think!”

    This simple but precise statement indicates that what we say, what we do, and what we feel – all have their origin in our mind.
    Hassles and problems are part of everyday life. Parents, friends, University, work, boy’/girl-friends, etc. – almost everything could create difficult times at some point. Luckily we can CHOOSE how to react to such situations.

    The energy of the human mind has great potential, and yet, often we underestimate its power. When we understand how we can use this power of our mind we would harness the energy that we possess. We would also be able to find the keys to happiness and contentment as well as to improving our relationships and circumstances.

    Positive thinking: Make Your Mind Your Best Friend

    How we think about ourselves and how we use our mind immediately affects our relationship both with ourselves and others. Trust, love, respect, understanding and good communication determine any good relationship. Are your own good friend and companion? If the answer is yes then automatically other people would ‘sense’ your attitude towards yourself and that would be the guidance for them to treat you accordingly.

    Thoughts are like seeds. Positive and happy thoughts produce enthusiastic and positive attitude towards everything you do. Otherwise thoughts can be anxious and depressing, producing painful sense of discomfort and worry. We can master our life by choosing to think positive thoughts which contain positive energy and optimistic, hopeful attitude. This almost certainly would lead us on the positive path to staying in control of our life, including dealing with some challenging times at University.

    Positive Thinking Vs Negative Thinking

    We can choose either to think a negative or positive, hopeful thoughts if we understand the very nature of a thought. We can learn methods for being more positive and constructive at home, at work and in our relationships.

    Simply try to look at your situation from a different angle. As if somebody would show you a front cover of a book but you would not see the back cover. To find out the other side of the story you would simply need to look at the situation from the unusual side. It is easier done when you talk to somebody who is not involved in the situation, for example, a student counsellor or your tutor to whom you trust.

    Try not to say or think things like ‘things always go wrong’ or ‘I never get it right’. There are usually lots of different reasons why things go not as we wish them to. If you are making critical remarks to yourself be as specific as possible. See setbacks of ‘failure’ as temporary, rather than permanent.

    Do remember: the way you think determines the way you feel and eventually the way you act. So thinking positively would almost certainly lead to a positive feeling and the positive outcome. Trust it! Even if it is a grey cloudy day outside it does not mean that there is no the sun behind those dark clouds. Keep doing what you are doing and a difficult situation will inevitably change with your determination.
    As it is said: “We are what we think”.
