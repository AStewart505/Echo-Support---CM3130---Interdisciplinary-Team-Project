    Sleep

    We require sleep to maintain our mental and physical wellbeing. The amount of sleep we need changes throughout our lifetime and most adults need on average seven to eight hours sleep per night. Difficulty sleeping or having disturbed sleep patterns could be due to stress or mental health problems. Lack of sleep occurring at exam time is normal for most people and a regular sleep pattern should resume once they are over. However, lack of sleep over a prolonged period of time can lead to fatigue and affect your concentration, or it may be a cause or a symptom of mental illness such as anxiety / depression or a physical ailment.

    Type of Sleep

    Deep sleep and rapid eye movement (REM) are the two types of sleep which we experience each time we sleep: deep sleep generally occurs at the beginning of the night and REM sleep happens the closer we get to wakening.

    Each cycle of sleep has its benefits: REM sleep is the time when we dream which allows us to process the experiences of the day and during deep sleep the body produces a growth hormone essential to the growth and repair our bodies.

    Most common sleep problems

    • Noise
    • Eating a large meal before bedtime
    • Drinking tea, coffee and smoking tobacco before bedtime
    • Temperature – too hot / cold
    • Medication
    • Stress
    • Anxiety / depression
    • A change in circumstances

    Improve the quality of your sleep

    • Go to bed when you are ready to sleep
    • Avoid using your computer or watching television in bed
    • If you can’t sleep after being in bed a short while, get up and try having a milky drink
    • Don’t begin to reflect on your day before bedtime
    • Don’t sleep in late to catch up after a disturbed night
    • Have a warm bath
    • Get enough exercise during the daytime
