    About This Website

    My name is Jo and I am one of the academic staff in the School of Engineering at RGU. I am also undertaking a PhD in the Mental Wellbeing of Engineering Students. As part of my research I discovered that engineering students and students in similar disciplines like computing and construction can suffer from very poor mental wellbeing but rarely ask for help.

    After some work with students and staff from 5 different universities, we developed this site, which is the start of building a useful resource for people who don’t like asking for help in the ways universities offer.

    It is very much a work in progress, and we are hoping that you can give us feedback on what works and what doesn’t, what could be added and what could be removed. It is a resource for you after all!

    On the webpages you will see quotes and facts from research papers looking at supporting mental health and wellbeing in engineering students (and others!). The suggestions and activities here are all supported by peer reviewed research projects, and the aim is to provide you with the know-how and support to improve or maintain your mental wellbeing and to give you confidence to know when to ask for more help if you need it.

    The site is hosted “off” RGU pages so you don’t have to log into Moodle to access them. While we do have some links to Moodle content that will require a login, we have no way to identify who you are. This means your visits here are confidential. We are collecting anonymous visit data like how often and when a page or item is accessed. We also have a feedback option if you would like to suggest improvements or tell us something that has worked well. This also is anonymous although we do ask for some demographic data to help with our report – it’s not compulsory to fill that in, but it helps!
